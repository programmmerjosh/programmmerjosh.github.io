App Development

Josh started getting into iOS development in 2016 shortly after having completed an introductory Computer Science course through Harvard University. Josh then was introduced to Xcode and the two main languages used in iOS development, Objective-C and Swift. It was at that moment; Josh decided that developing iPhone and iPad apps was what he wanted to do. Starting with Objective-C, Josh swiftly moved over to Swift and found it to be the more suitable and broadly utilised choice across the board. Four years later, Josh has managed to produce a number of different apps ranging from simple calculation apps to machine learning to augmented reality. Josh has grown in leaps and bounds, but his development journey doesn't end there. As of July 2020, Josh has invested in time and education to transition over to cross platform app development using Google's Flutter. Now the limitations of just the Apple market are no more. Being able to cater to the Apple as well as the Android market has opened up a lot more opportunities and potential.

Web Development

Josh's exposure to web development goes a bit further back. He began learning the fundamentals of HTML, CSS, JS and SQL in 2013, continuing through 2015 when he studied Computer Science and IT in South Africa. Despite having learnt a lot in those three years of studies, Josh endeavoured to broaden his skill set by learning and pursuing mobile application development. Nevertheless, when 2020 came around, there were a few web development opportunities that arose. So, Josh made the decision to relearn, update some old skills and build upon them by jumping right into learning Bootstrap, jQuery, Vue and Node. It would be fair to say that Josh is very confident in his ability and very capable, at least when it comes to front-end development. Josh's back-end skills may not strech as far yet, but there's of course nothing a person can't learn. It should go without saying that the current website you're on is Josh's work. Not bad ey.

Investing

For a number of years now, Josh has dabbled in trading crypto currencies and common stocks. It has become apparent that trading cannot be confused with investing. Josh has grown in his understanding of the market and its fluctuations. He is no longer seeking quick optimistic and unrealistic returns. He understands that no-one can accurately and consistenlty predict exactly when a particular market or stock will go up or down. Josh now focuses on long term portfolio investments, financial education and soon to be working on passive income by means of real estate investments.
